Physics 11

For many students this will be the first course they have ever taken which moves extensively into the connection between science, engineering, and mathematics. It's important that this course work to install critical thinking and questioning in students, and also develop their organizing, problem solving, and collaborative skills. The overarching units in the course are as follows,

  • Kinematics
  • Dynamics
  • Momentum, Impulse, and Collisions
  • Work and Power
  • Energy and Efficiency

Momentum, Impulse, and Collisions

This unit begins with a derivation of Newton's original second law, exploring what he described as the quantity of motion. We then look at the impulse momentum theorem, and connect that to conservation of momentum. Finally we investigate collisions and explosions.

Work, Power, and Energy

More often than not I combine the work and power unit into the energy and efficiency unit. We begin with the idea of work and power, then extend this to the Work-Energy Theorem, Conservation of Mechanical Energy, and finally Conservation of Energy.
