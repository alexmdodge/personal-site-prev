Rotations

This is a video I created on rotations. This is a unit with which grade 8 students struggle a great deal with. Translations and reflections often make sense, as the steps to perform them are a bit more straight forward, but rotations involve more difficult steps, with tricky vocabulary to accompany it. In this video I had them follow along using a sheet I created, which I will link in the course page of my site.

Geo-Strips

Geo-Strips are an incredibly useful math manipulative which you can use with students of all levels. The strips themselves aren't complicated to set up, but it's important that students understand that the strips are consistent sizes, and if you use them for trigonometry, then you must attach them at the ends. This video was meant more as a supplementary video for students who were having troubles with the activities.

The Bullseye Compass

When drawing circles or arcs, the traditional compass is a bit outdated. On top of that, it can be unreliable and produce inconsistent circles. In this video I go through a number of different types of Bullseye Compasses, and how to use them. They can also be used for angle measurements by observing the sweep of a drawn arc.

Rulers and Protractors

For some of my students on IPPs and adaptations, the ruler and the protractor presented a great deal of difficulty. This video is meant as a primer for those who have not used a ruler or protractor before (or for a long time). For a ruler, students more often than not start at 1, and on the protractor there is is a lot of difficulty knowing which side to start measuring from.

Electrostatics Solutions

In my physics 12 course I taught electrostatics, and I noticed that I was getting constant questions from students for more practice. In this video I stepped through a number of different electrostatics problems. The hardest thing for students in this unit is the distinction between electric potential, and electric potential energy.
