07/03/2014 - First Day of Class

To say I was excited for this course is an understatment. I believe that technology is an incredibly important part of society, and it is only going to become more prevalent as time goes on. When I was first reading the course outline I was very excited by the fact that I would have the opportunity to develop my professional profile. I love graphic design, web design, document preparation, and a host of other technologies. I look forward to using all these skills to hep forward my own goal of becoming a teacher.

Today in class we talked about the various technologies we would use throughout the course. I took a survey which helped outline my knowledge surrounding different pieces of software. I think in this course I will place focus on the online markup software, as this has the greatest potential for interactive programming you could do as a class. These include programs like google documents, and office online. I look forward to tomorrow's lesson, where we will start to look into our web pages.

07/04/2014 - Up and Running

We started today by re-capping the information from yesterday, as well as getting into our groups and discussing topics for our presentations. The group I am a part of decided to focus on interactive technologies. The idea I had was surrounding motioni capture and virtual reality based hardware. It would be incredible to give your students a tour of the ISS like they were actually there, or show them the Grand Canyon first hand. There are limitless capabilities with technology, and we just have to decide where best to put it in an education setting.

Afterwards we had a brief introduction to google sites, and were sent on our ways. Initially I was open to the idea of using google sites, but soon realized it was failry limited in terms of its features. The most powerful web hosting/design site I'm aware of is wordpress. I think having the general populace versed or at least aware of web design aspects is so critical, because everything is online nowadays. When everyone knows this basic knowledge, the quality, content, and security of online content becomes monumentally better. In the end I decided to take a custome design approach, as I had many ideas which I was not able to convey in the google sites application.

07/05/2014 - Introduction to Graphic Organizers

We began today by reviewing what we have done in our groups to this point. We have decided to do interactive classroom technologies as mentioned in the previous entry. We then reviewed some individual ePortfolio's of my fellow classmates. Some of the google sites pages looked quite good! We then lookedin uploading techniques for our resume's and collected works. For my portfolio, to reduce the size of the content I have stored on online servers, I'll reference links to my dropbox which will have dedicated sections for content. Overtime I hope to integrate bloggin software, as well as some javascript and videos into my site.

For the remainder of the class we looked into the graphic organizing software Inspiration, as well as other free to use software. I was testing the online application called Webspiration. From my use of the software before, and the prelimary testing of the online version, my preferred software of choice is an open source program known as Dia. This has many of the same options, but in some ways is better, as it has many add-ons freely available for download. It also works with Linux based operating systems. I believe one of my educational goals will be to help linux based OS make its way into the schooling system a bit more. At time it can be troublesome, but it gives a whole other level of understanding to those with the patience to try it.

07/07/2014 - Hurrican Surprise

Well the hurricane certainly made this an interesting weekend, but I'm lucky in the fact that my power came back on Sunday. Today, due to the cancellation, I worked on the online assignments designated for the online class day. I now realize that having a class like this can be extremely useful. I know that you can't just give students a day off in public school to go work at home, but the idea of always planning for school cancellations is such an important idea. Because of that extra class, we were able to recover from the day off, and continue the course as planned without a hitch. Backup planning in education seems essential. Always having just that little bit extra to do allows you so much more flexibility in your lessons, no matter what goes wrong. I think a good way to account for days off is have pieces of lessons that even each day can be cut out if necessary, to help catch up. This could be a quick reflection at the end of class, or some other piece which has its place, but can be substituted if necessary.

07/08/2014 - Stop Motion Video Creation

Today we created stop motion videos using cameras, and whatever else we could get out hands on. We ended up creating a paper animation with some random toys each of us brought in. The activity was a lot of fun, and let us experiment with our cameras, and the stock video software provided on our computers.

The most important thing I took away from this activity is giving students a chance to let their creativity shine. We had three and a half hours to work on our projects, and we may have a bit experience in terms of creation, but by spreading this activity over a couple of classes it would be incredible to see what students would come up with. Giving youth the ability to direct their own learning by drawing on their own knowledge and interests makes the whole process so much more rich and exciting. The end products are also much higher quality because the students feel what they've created has more personal value.

07/09/2014 - Open Workshop Project

Today was focused around finishing any outstanding assignments as well as working on our group projects for the final class. One thing I was initially dreading, but have come to love, are all the group presentations we've done so far.

Today was another good example of a class where it's student directed. During these days you can simply leave students with something to work on, whether it be assigned work, or a self-directed project, and it gives them a chance to reflect a bit more on what they're working on, as well as determining where each student in the class is. Inidividual and group work is so important because it is transactional learning where students are given the opportunity to communicate their own ideas, instead of being presented someone elses. Asking a student why is certainly one of my central beliefs in the category of student centered learning.

07/10/2014 - Copyright Introduction

I was particularly excited to have this class. As a aspiring web designer, information property is a very touchy subject, and I needed some sort of introduction. I looked on the internet for information but I was never one hundred percent of what I was reading. I think I have a much better grasp now on what copyright is, and feel comfortable in how this pertains to my own personal work, and work that I use from other sources.

I liked the format of this class because it required us to not only learn the material but also apply it immediately in the context of our classroom. This is another tier of my philosophy, which is practical application. A concept will not stick with a student unless they know why they're learning it, where to use it, or where it will be used eventually. As the saying goes, practice makes perfect, and you can never just deliver a topic to a student and expect them to remember it the first time.

07/11/2014 - Instructional Technology Presentation

Today was the first of the three IT presentation groups. The goal of these groups was to collaborate, collect, and present information on technology concerning education. This is another incredibly powerful teaching method. As a teacher you are one mind, and while you may have access and knowledge of a greater number of resources, you don't have access to more creativity or inovation. This is where self-directed and group collaboration work so well, and can enrich not only your curriculum, but teach their peers something as well.

Of all the presentations today I felt the most useful was technology tools for the classroom. It is good to talk about issues, but once you understand them, repeating similar arugments over and over does little to provide new information. This group provided helpful applications and resources one might use in their class for presentations, modelling, or recording. This was all very helpful and I will no doubt use some of the applications in my future classroom.

07/14/2014 - Internet Awareness Presentation

Today the focus was the internet, or the danger which lies within it. This is a very interesting subject, as I have not had direct responsibility for a young child in contact with the internet, so I am nieve as to the types of difficulties one might encounter. I found the most useful presentation for me was the pinterest board with links to various resources about cyberbullying. Cyberbullying is becoming ever more prevalent, and dangerous, because of the technology available to the general populace. This is something I think everyone could use a little support in, and I'm so happy this team put together a collection of so many useful resources.

I think early incororaption of technology into classrooms, and continued reinforcement of proper practices is the only way to deal with misuse of technology. Another thing to remember is that we have practices to deal with bullying, and it still happens. It's something that will always happyen. So instead of going in with the mindset that it's something to eradicate, we need to go in with the mindset that it will always be there and how do we minimize it. I think the laws the government has put into place are going to help a lot, but what is going to change things is a more proactive approach to technology orientation within schools. If students don't know how to use it properly, then we aren't doing our jobs correctly. Obviously there will be some external cases, and that's why there are laws.
