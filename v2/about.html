Welcome to my online portfolio! I have done my best to reliably create and organize all the information on this site. If anything is out of place, or something is not working, please leave me a message through the contact page.

Teaching is something that I have always done, and plan to do for the rest of my life. When I was young I had a habit of trying to help my siblings with everything in their life. I loved working with my friends to solve problems, and I could almost always be found with a pile of Lego in my lap. Swimming has always been an avenue of growth for me. I started swimming lessons when I was young, and moved on to take higher level lifesaving courses. I eventually became a swim instructor and lifeguard. I loved teaching swimming lessons, and still teach from time to time. Eventually upon reaching university I started to tutor, helped with first year physics classes, and obtained certifications to teach lifeguards.

I attended Acadia University for my undergraduate degree, and finished with a double major in physics and computer science. Typically the field path for either of these degrees is to take an honours option, and then continue to a masters degree. The problem was, I just wasn't satisfied with that idea. When I was deciding about what I wanted to do, I couldn't settle on a specific field. I just loved it all too much. That's when it started to dawn on me that there was so much to learn, I never wanted to stop. I wanted to share, I wanted to explore, I wanted to teach.

I currently have teachables in physics and math with methods courses in technology education. I have a deep passion for technology, and in all of my classes I will work to integrate technology effectively into the classroom. The most important thing about technology is understanding when it should be used. If there is an instance where a pencil and paper would be more appropriate, then its best to use that. Forcing technology results in misuse.

In my spare time I love to swim, program, travel, and cook. Travelling is one of my other passions. I've been across Canada, around the US, across the pond to the British Isles and France, and down to Africa. I consider myself very lucky in that I've had the opportunity to see all of these places. Travel gives you the chance to see and experience another culture. This is so incredibly important because it helps to break the bubble we all inevitibaly live in, being in one place for so long. I've found that travel has helped to define my belief of community, and allowed me to appreciate everything in my life more and more.

I hope this site is found to be informative and useful. I end this blurb with one of my favorite quotes. "Live as if you were to die tomorrow, learn as if you were to live forever." - Mahatma Gandhi
