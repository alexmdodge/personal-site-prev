My teaching style is built on a three tier system, which covers all aspects of a classroom, school, and community of learners. My belief is that teaching should be child centered, incorporate diversity, and inspire true motivation.

Student-Centered
This tier covers many aspects in the classroom. First and foremost it means that a youth's needs come first. This includes every student in every class. Whether that student has a disability, trouble with a subject, or is experiencing difficulty in some other way, their needs come first and I will dedicate time to help them in whatever capacity I can. Youth cannot learn without their basic needs met. Secondly, this means that students must be given an opportunity to interact in the class, and are repeatedly asked why. If we don't question students, they leave school believing their is nothing in society to question, and therefore nothing to change. Finally this also means that students must help derive the curriculum in some way. This means including self-study topics, open projects, and goal setting. Giving students the agency to direct their learning not only inspires strong creative content, but also encourages a strong collaboration of cross-curricular material.

Diverse Classrooms
This tier involves themes of community, society, culture, religion, inclusion, and collaboration. The most important thing you can teach a child is that as much as life can be competitive, the most powerful creations come about from a team effort. Teaching students about the connection to their local and global community allows them to place themselves in the context of the world, and obtain a much larger perspective in terms of their global opinions. I believe a class which promotes equality over neutrality is key. Everyone's religions, cultures, and societies should be respected and treated as equal, but at the same time compromise is necessary in a truly multicultural setting. Instead of pinning students against each other it is essential to pose class wide, community wide, and global goals. The sense of accomplishment a group of students get from true collaboration and friendship is the most powerful reinforcement in teaching.

True Motivation
Too often when I think back to high school, I found myself questioning why I was learning certain subjects. Looking back I can now see why I learned some things but not others. At the time I was lost, and if certain teachers had given a more concrete application, real life connection, or legitimate reason behind the content, I would have been more strongly motivated. When teaching a subject its not just as easy to say you must learn it. If a curriculum includes something, it has been put there for a reason and it is the responsibility of the teacher to reveal those reasons. It should not be a secret to your students. Practical explanation, application, and practice are key when teaching any subject. Even if a theory is abstract, or doesn't have any near future uses, you must stress the importance of what it could lead to later on. I believe that demonstration is another powerful tool to help students in the path of learning. I aim to provide a rich curriculum which not only teach theoretical content, but also shows the practical application of it as well.
